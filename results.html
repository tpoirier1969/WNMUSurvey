Decision Brief

Rules-based findings surface the strongest decision signals in the current filtered view without replacing the underlying evidence.

How findings are generated and limited
  • No finding is shown without its answered, paired, or module denominator.
  • Core findings require at least five usable answers for the stated measure.
  • Optional follow-up findings always identify the voluntary, self-selected module population.
  • Synthetic records remain labeled as test data and are not research conclusions.
  • Practical responses are presented as options to investigate, not automatic directives.
  • Filters recalculate the findings; small filtered groups may produce no finding.
